ABOUT ME

Howdy! I'm Sierra. Born and bred in the military town that is Colorado Springs, my grandfather was an army veteran and my grandmother is a Korean immigrant- I know a thing or five about resilience.

  • Name: Sierra Nash
  • Age: 26
  • Pronouns: She/They
  • Assets: Methodical, Acute, Candid
  • Drawbacks: Anxious, Stoical, Self-denying
  • Cool Info: "Alpha wolves" don't exist in the wild! source

My love for engineering and computer science stems, pun intended, from my bewilderment at my Black father's pursuit of a degree in the field when I was little. It seems ignorant, but I genuinely didn't know Black people could do that ... succeed in STEM. Ironically, he's not a model dad and it was my "ghetto" (his words, not mine) Korean/Italian mother and her family who raised me and pushed me to explore. While some say ghetto, I say no-nonsense, resourceful, and innovative. I dabbled in STEM classes throughout high school and had the privilege of attending the National Youth Leadership Forum (NYLF): Careers in Engineering & Technology Envision program in the summer of 2014. I tried to major in computer science in college but failed calculus during my first semester despite hours of tutoring. I changed my major and ditched my passion for STEM, temporarily. Fast-forward 7 years and I'm a graduate of the Justice Through Code (JTC) full stack web development intensive as of July 2022.
